3.1 How to improve fast charging capability

This section explains the mechanism behind fast charging, including lithium-ion kinetics and thermal management.

3.1.1 Optimizing lithium ion migration paths

In most cases, reducing particle size, reducing surface density, and reducing compaction density (increasing porosity) can effectively accelerate the migration path of lithium ions.

Fick's Second Law:

\[ \frac{\partial C}{\partial t} = D \frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial x^2} \]

figure 4：Fast-charging cycle life(45℃ 10%-80%SOC，Different particle sizes and different surface densities)*

3.1.2 Reduce polarization during charge and discharge

Polarization usually comes from the increase of internal resistance, which requires a larger overvoltage to drive and will end the charge and discharge faster.

Butler–Volmer equation(Characterizing electrochemical polarization):

\[ j = j_0 \left( e^{\frac{\alpha_a n F \eta}{RT}} - e^{-\frac{\alpha_c n F \eta}{RT}} \right) \]

The Nernst equation(Characterizing concentration polarization):

\[ E = E^\circ - \frac{RT}{nF} \ln \left( \frac{C_o}{C_r} \right) \]

3.1.3 Optimizing tab design

During charging, the current density on the negative tab side is the highest and the potential is the lowest, so lithium will be deposited first,Properly increase the number of tabs to make the current distribution more uniform.
